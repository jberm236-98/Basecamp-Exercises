# Path Cards

You diagnose **together** first — read the email, find what's actually breaking it, and prove to yourselves it isn't the model. Then split. Pick the path that plays to your strength.

---

## Technical path — own the fix

**You get:** the running system and the scoreboard.

**You do:** fix the broken levers — prompts, tools, routing — so the agent actually resolves tickets. You may **not** change the model. Rerun the eval after every change.

**You produce:** a fixed system with the before-and-after score, plus the cost number. The proof.

**Lands in the brief:** *The fix* and *Proof*.

> Push yourself: can you get the cost *down* too — same quality, fewer dollars? Look at what the coordinator sends to the model on every single turn.

---

## Commercial path — own the client

**You get:** the diagnosis the team found, and the numbers as the technical path produces them.

**You do:** turn it into something Priya can act on. Build the story — what was breaking it (plain English, no jargon), what the fix changes, and why it was never the model.

**You produce:** the one-page client brief ([client-brief-template.md](client-brief-template.md)) and a 2-minute demo: the same ticket, broken vs. fixed, score and cost side by side.

**Lands in the brief:** *What's breaking it*, *What it would take*, and *The objection*.

> You own the hardest question in the room. When Priya's CFO asks *"why don't we just pay for a better model?"*, you answer it — with your own numbers. This is the deliverable the room sees at the end.
